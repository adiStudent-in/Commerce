1. 🏛️ Role of Business in Development of Economy

India's Ancient Trade (Key Facts)

  • Trading via Silk Route and maritime trade were the main routes
  • India had the largest economy in the ancient/medieval world between 1st–7th centuries CE
  • Controlled 1/3 to 1/4 of the world's wealth
  • India was called 'Swaran Bhoomi and Swaran Deep' by travellers (Megasthenes, FaHien, Huen Tsang, Al Beruni, Ibn Batuta, Francois)

British Era Impact

  • East India Company used Indian revenues to buy Indian raw materials
  • India shifted from exporter of processed goods → exporter of raw materials + buyer of manufactured goods

Post-Independence

  • Emphasis on public investment in basic industries

Current India

  • Initiatives: Make in India, Skill India, Digital India

2. 💡 Concept of Business

Business = an economic activity involving production and sale of goods/services with a motive of earning profit by satisfying human needs.
The term 'business' is derived from the word 'busy'.

Types of Human Activities

TypeMeaningExample
EconomicDone to earn livelihoodWorker in factory, doctor in clinic
Non-economicDone out of love, sympathy, emotionHousewife cooking for family, helping old man

Economic Activities (3 Types)

  1. Business – production/sale of goods/services for profit
  2. Profession – personalised expert services (e.g., doctor, lawyer)
  3. Employment – work done as per service contract for salary

3. ✅ Characteristics of Business Activities (7 Points)

#CharacteristicExplanation
iEconomic activityDone for earning money/livelihood, not out of emotion
iiProduction or procurementGoods must be produced or procured before being sold
iiiSale or exchangeMust involve transfer of goods/services for value
ivRegular dealingsOne single transaction ≠ business; must be on regular basis
vProfit earningMain purpose is to earn profit; no business survives without profit
viUncertainty of returnFuture profit/loss not certain; capital at risk
viiElement of riskRisk = uncertainty associated with exposure to loss; can't be eliminated
💡 Key Example: Cooking food at home = NOT business. Cooking & selling in restaurant = Business.
Selling old radio set once = NOT business. Selling radio sets regularly = Business.

5.1 🏭 Industry (Detailed)

Industry = economic activities connected with conversion of resources into useful goods.

Primary Industries

TypeMeaningExamples
ExtractiveExtract products from natural sourcesFarming, mining, lumbering, hunting, fishing
GeneticBreeding plants/animals for reproductionSeeds/nursery companies, cattle breeding, poultry farms, fish hatchery

Secondary Industries

TypeMeaningExamples
ManufacturingProcess raw materials into finished goodsSugar, paper, cement, oil refinery, TV, car
ConstructionConstruction of structuresBuildings, dams, bridges, roads, tunnels, canals

4 Types of Manufacturing Industries:

  • Analytical – separates elements from same material (oil refinery)
  • Synthetical – combines ingredients into new product (cement)
  • Processing – successive stages for finished products (sugar, paper)
  • Assembling – assembles component parts (TV, car, computer)

Tertiary Industries

  • Provide support services to primary and secondary industries
  • Include: transport, banking, insurance, warehousing, communication, packaging, advertising
  • Also considered part of commerce (auxiliaries to trade)

5.2 🛒 Commerce (Detailed)

Commerce = Trade + Auxiliaries to Trade
Purpose: Remove hindrances in exchange of goods and services

Hindrances Removed by Commerce

HindranceRemoved byHow
PersonsTradeMakes goods available from producers to consumers
PlaceTransportMoves goods from production to markets
TimeStorage/WarehousingHolds goods until required
RiskInsuranceProtects against loss/damage
FinanceBankingProvides capital for business
InformationAdvertisingInforms consumers about goods/services

Types of Trade

  • Internal Trade: Within the country
    • Wholesale trade
    • Retail trade
  • External Trade: Between countries
    • Import
    • Export
    • Entrepot (re-export)

5.3 🔗 Auxiliaries to Trade (5 Types)

AuxiliaryHindrance RemovedRole
Transport & CommunicationPlaceMoves raw materials to production; finished goods to consumers; postal/telephone for communication
Banking & FinanceFinanceProvides overdraft, loans, advances; collects cheques; remits funds; discounts bills; helps exporters
InsuranceRiskProtects against fire, theft, accidents; employees protected from occupational hazards; nominal premium paid
WarehousingTimeStores goods until needed; maintains price stability through continuous supply
Advertising & PRInformationAdvertising = paid (space in media); PR = unpaid (strategic communication, press releases in print/social media)
💡 Key distinction: Advertising is always paid. PR activities are normally unpaid.

6. 🎯 Objectives of Business

Profit is the leading objective but NOT the only objective.

Why Profit is Important:

  • Source of income for business persons
  • Finance for expansion
  • Indicates efficient working
  • Society's approval of business utility
  • Builds reputation

6 Objectives of Business

#ObjectiveMeaning
iMarket StandingMaintaining goodwill/reputation; forming distinct identity vs competitors
iiInnovationIntroducing new ideas/methods; 2 types: (a) innovation in product/services and (b) innovation in skills/activities
iiiProductivityOutput value vs input value; measure of efficiency; best use of resources
ivPhysical & Financial ResourcesAcquiring plants, machines, offices, funds efficiently
vEarning ProfitsProfitability = profit in relation to capital investment; necessary for survival & growth
viSocial ResponsibilityContributing resources for solving social problems; working in socially desirable manner

7. ⚠️ Business Risk

Business Risk = possibility of inadequate profits or losses due to uncertainties or unexpected events.

Two Types of Business Risk

TypeMeaningExample
Speculative RiskPossibility of gain OR lossChanges in demand/supply, price fluctuations, fashion changes
Pure RiskPossibility of loss only (no gain if not occurs)Fire, theft, strike

7.1 Nature of Business Risks (4 Points)

#Characteristic
iRisk is essential part of every business – can be minimised but NOT eliminated
iiRisks arise due to uncertainties – natural calamities, demand changes, govt. policy changes
iiiDegree of risk depends on nature and size of business – fashion goods = high risk; large scale = higher risk
ivProfit is reward for risk taking – 'No risk, no gain'; greater risk = higher chance of profit

7.2 Causes of Business Risks (4 Types)

CauseExamples
Natural causesFlood, earthquake, lightning, heavy rains, famine
Human causesDishonesty, negligence of employees, strikes, riots, power failure, management inefficiency
Economic causesDemand changes, competition, price changes, change in technology, rise in interest rate, higher taxes
Other causesPolitical disturbances, mechanical failures (bursting of boiler), exchange rate fluctuations

8. 🚀 Starting a Business – Basic Factors (8 Factors)

Entrepreneurship = process of setting up one's own business
Entrepreneur = person who sets up the business
Business Enterprise = the output/business unit
#FactorKey Points
iSelection of type of businessNature/type to undertake; influenced by customer requirements + technical knowledge/interest of entrepreneur
iiSize of businessMSME or large scale? Manufacturing or tertiary? Depends on demand confidence and available capital
iiiLocation of businessAffects cost of production; consider raw materials, labour, power supply, banking, transport, communication, warehousing
ivFinancing the propositionCapital for fixed assets (land, building, machinery) + current assets (raw materials, stock) + day-to-day expenses
vPhysical facilitiesMachines, equipment, building, supportive services; depends on nature/size of business and production process
viCompetent & committed workforceIdentify requirement of skilled/unskilled workers; plan training and motivation
viiTax planningConsider tax liability under various laws; impacts all aspects of business functioning
viiiLaunching the enterpriseMobilise resources, fulfil legal formalities, start production, initiate sales promotion; decide (a) capital requirement (b) source of capital (c) best use of capital
