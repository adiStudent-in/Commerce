## 1. 🏛️ Role of Business in Development of Economy

### India's Ancient Trade (Key Facts)
- Trading via **Silk Route** and **maritime trade** were the main routes
- India had the **largest economy** in the ancient/medieval world between 1st–7th centuries CE
- Controlled **1/3 to 1/4 of the world's wealth**
- India was called **'Swaran Bhoomi and Swaran Deep'** by travellers (Megasthenes, FaHien, Huen Tsang, Al Beruni, Ibn Batuta, Francois)

### Major Exports (Ancient India)
Spices, wheat, sugar, indigo, opium, sesame oil, cotton, parrots, live animals, animal products

### Major Imports (Ancient India)
Horses, animal products, Chinese silks, linen, wine, gold, silver, copper

### British Era Impact
- East India Company used Indian revenues to buy Indian raw materials
- India shifted from **exporter of processed goods → exporter of raw materials + buyer of manufactured goods**

### Post-Independence
- Planned development with centralised economic planning
- Emphasis on public investment in basic industries
- Problems: lack of capital, rising population, weak financial system, high fiscal deficit
- **1991: Economic Liberalisation** → Three-pronged: stabilisation, restructuring, globalisation

### Current India
- Preferred **FDI destination**
- Initiatives: **Make in India**, **Skill India**, **Digital India**

## 2. 💡 Concept of Business

> **Business** = an economic activity involving **production and sale of goods/services** with a **motive of earning profit** by satisfying human needs.

> The term 'business' is derived from the word **'busy'**.

### Types of Human Activities

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Economic | Done to earn livelihood | Worker in factory, doctor in clinic |
| Non-economic | Done out of love, sympathy, emotion | Housewife cooking for family, helping old man |

### Economic Activities (3 Types)
1. **Business** – production/sale of goods/services for profit
2. **Profession** – personalised expert services (e.g., doctor, lawyer)
3. **Employment** – work done as per service contract for salary

---

## 3. ✅ Characteristics of Business Activities (7 Points)

| # | Characteristic | Explanation |
|---|---------------|-------------|
| i | Economic activity | Done for earning money/livelihood, not out of emotion |
| ii | Production or procurement | Goods must be produced or procured before being sold |
| iii | Sale or exchange | Must involve transfer of goods/services for value |
| iv | Regular dealings | One single transaction ≠ business; must be on regular basis |
| v | Profit earning | Main purpose is to earn profit; no business survives without profit |
| vi | Uncertainty of return | Future profit/loss not certain; capital at risk |
| vii | Element of risk | Risk = uncertainty associated with exposure to loss; can't be eliminated |

> 💡 Key Example: Cooking food at home = NOT business. Cooking & selling in restaurant = Business.  
> Selling old radio set once = NOT business. Selling radio sets regularly = Business.

## 4. 📊 Business vs Profession vs Employment

| Basis | Business | Profession | Employment |
|-------|---------|------------|------------|
| Mode of establishment | Entrepreneur's decision + legal formalities | Membership of professional body + certificate of practice | Appointment letter + service agreement |
| Nature of work | Provision of goods/services to public | Personalised expert services | Work as per service contract |
| Qualification | No minimum required | Prescribed qualifications must | As prescribed by employer |
| Reward/Return | **Profit** | **Professional fee** | **Salary/wages** |
| Capital investment | Required as per size/nature | Limited capital needed | No capital required |
| Risk | Uncertain, irregular; high risk | Fee generally regular; some risk | Fixed, regular; no/little risk |
| Transfer of interest | Possible with formalities | Not possible | Not possible |
| Code of conduct | No code prescribed | Professional code must be followed | Employer's norms must be followed |
| Example | Shop, factory | Legal, medical, CA | Bank jobs, government jobs |

## 5.1 🏭 Industry (Detailed)

> Industry = economic activities connected with **conversion of resources into useful goods**.

### Primary Industries
| Type | Meaning | Examples |
|------|---------|---------|
| Extractive | Extract products from natural sources | Farming, mining, lumbering, hunting, fishing |
| Genetic | Breeding plants/animals for reproduction | Seeds/nursery companies, cattle breeding, poultry farms, fish hatchery |

### Secondary Industries
| Type | Meaning | Examples |
|------|---------|---------|
| Manufacturing | Process raw materials into finished goods | Sugar, paper, cement, oil refinery, TV, car |
| Construction | Construction of structures | Buildings, dams, bridges, roads, tunnels, canals |

**4 Types of Manufacturing Industries:**
- **Analytical** – separates elements from same material (oil refinery)
- **Synthetical** – combines ingredients into new product (cement)
- **Processing** – successive stages for finished products (sugar, paper)
- **Assembling** – assembles component parts (TV, car, computer)

### Tertiary Industries
- Provide support services to primary and secondary industries
- Include: transport, banking, insurance, warehousing, communication, packaging, advertising
- Also considered part of **commerce** (auxiliaries to trade)

---

## 5.2 🛒 Commerce (Detailed)

> Commerce = **Trade + Auxiliaries to Trade**  
> Purpose: Remove hindrances in exchange of goods and services

### Hindrances Removed by Commerce

| Hindrance | Removed by | How |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Persons | Trade | Makes goods available from producers to consumers |
| Place | Transport | Moves goods from production to markets |
| Time | Storage/Warehousing | Holds goods until required |
| Risk | Insurance | Protects against loss/damage |
| Finance | Banking | Provides capital for business |
| Information | Advertising | Informs consumers about goods/services |

### Types of Trade
- **Internal Trade:** Within the country
  - Wholesale trade
  - Retail trade
- **External Trade:** Between countries
  - Import
  - Export
  - Entrepot (re-export)

---

## 5.3 🔗 Auxiliaries to Trade (5 Types)

| Auxiliary | Hindrance Removed | Role |
|-----------|------------------|------|
| **Transport & Communication** | Place | Moves raw materials to production; finished goods to consumers; postal/telephone for communication |
| **Banking & Finance** | Finance | Provides overdraft, loans, advances; collects cheques; remits funds; discounts bills; helps exporters |
| **Insurance** | Risk | Protects against fire, theft, accidents; employees protected from occupational hazards; nominal premium paid |
| **Warehousing** | Time | Stores goods until needed; maintains price stability through continuous supply |
| **Advertising & PR** | Information | Advertising = **paid** (space in media); PR = **unpaid** (strategic communication, press releases in print/social media) |

> 💡 Key distinction: **Advertising is always paid. PR activities are normally unpaid.**

## 6. 🎯 Objectives of Business

> Profit is the **leading** objective but **NOT the only** objective.

### Why Profit is Important:
- Source of income for business persons
- Finance for expansion
- Indicates efficient working
- Society's approval of business utility
- Builds reputation

### 6 Objectives of Business

| # | Objective | Meaning |
|---|-----------|---------|
| i | **Market Standing** | Maintaining goodwill/reputation; forming distinct identity vs competitors |
| ii | **Innovation** | Introducing new ideas/methods; 2 types: (a) innovation in product/services and (b) innovation in skills/activities |
| iii | **Productivity** | Output value vs input value; measure of efficiency; best use of resources |
| iv | **Physical & Financial Resources** | Acquiring plants, machines, offices, funds efficiently |
| v | **Earning Profits** | Profitability = profit in relation to capital investment; necessary for survival & growth |
| vi | **Social Responsibility** | Contributing resources for solving social problems; working in socially desirable manner |

## 7. ⚠️ Business Risk

> **Business Risk** = possibility of **inadequate profits or losses** due to uncertainties or unexpected events.

### Two Types of Business Risk

| Type | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Speculative Risk** | Possibility of **gain OR loss** | Changes in demand/supply, price fluctuations, fashion changes |
| **Pure Risk** | Possibility of **loss only** (no gain if not occurs) | Fire, theft, strike |

### 7.1 Nature of Business Risks (4 Points)

| # | Characteristic |
|---|----------------|
| i | **Risk is essential part of every business** – can be minimised but NOT eliminated |
| ii | **Risks arise due to uncertainties** – natural calamities, demand changes, govt. policy changes |
| iii | **Degree of risk depends on nature and size of business** – fashion goods = high risk; large scale = higher risk |
| iv | **Profit is reward for risk taking** – 'No risk, no gain'; greater risk = higher chance of profit |

### 7.2 Causes of Business Risks (4 Types)

| Cause | Examples |
|-------|---------|
| **Natural causes** | Flood, earthquake, lightning, heavy rains, famine |
| **Human causes** | Dishonesty, negligence of employees, strikes, riots, power failure, management inefficiency |
| **Economic causes** | Demand changes, competition, price changes, change in technology, rise in interest rate, higher taxes |
| **Other causes** | Political disturbances, mechanical failures (bursting of boiler), exchange rate fluctuations |

## 8. 🚀 Starting a Business – Basic Factors (8 Factors)

> **Entrepreneurship** = process of setting up one's own business  
> **Entrepreneur** = person who sets up the business  
> **Business Enterprise** = the output/business unit

| # | Factor | Key Points |
|---|--------|-----------|
| i | **Selection of type of business** | Nature/type to undertake; influenced by customer requirements + technical knowledge/interest of entrepreneur |
| ii | **Size of business** | MSME or large scale? Manufacturing or tertiary? Depends on demand confidence and available capital |
| iii | **Location of business** | Affects cost of production; consider raw materials, labour, power supply, banking, transport, communication, warehousing |
| iv | **Financing the proposition** | Capital for fixed assets (land, building, machinery) + current assets (raw materials, stock) + day-to-day expenses |
| v | **Physical facilities** | Machines, equipment, building, supportive services; depends on nature/size of business and production process |
| vi | **Competent & committed workforce** | Identify requirement of skilled/unskilled workers; plan training and motivation |
| vii | **Tax planning** | Consider tax liability under various laws; impacts all aspects of business functioning |
| viii | **Launching the enterprise** | Mobilise resources, fulfil legal formalities, start production, initiate sales promotion; decide (a) capital requirement (b) source of capital (c) best use of capital |

## 📝 Key Terms to Remember

- **Hundi** – Ancient indigenous instrument of exchange; means 'to collect'; written in vernacular language
- **Karkhanas** – Family-based workshops for manufacturing (ancient India)
- **Entrepreneurship** – Process of setting up own business
- **Entrepreneur** – Person who sets up business; becomes job-provider not job-seeker
- **Speculative Risk** – Has both possibility of gain and loss
- **Pure Risk** – Only possibility of loss (fire, theft, strike)
- **Auxiliaries to Trade** – Activities supporting trade (transport, banking, insurance, warehousing, advertising)
- **Entrepot Trade** – Import of goods for re-export to other countries
- **Productivity** – Output value ÷ Input value; measure of efficiency
- **Innovation** – Introduction of new ideas/methods (in product/service OR in skills/activities)
