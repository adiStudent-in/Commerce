## 1. 🧍 Sole Proprietorship

> Owned, managed and controlled by **one individual** who bears all risks and receives all profits.

### Features
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Formation & Closure | No separate law; minimal legal formalities; easy to start & close |
| Liability | **Unlimited** – personal assets can be used to pay business debts |
| Risk & Profit | Sole risk bearer AND sole profit recipient |
| Control | Absolute control with the owner |
| Separate Entity | **No** – owner and business are the same in law |
| Continuity | **No** – death/insanity/bankruptcy ends the business |

### ✅ Merits
- Quick decision making
- Confidentiality of information (not bound to publish accounts)
- Direct incentive (keeps all profits)
- Sense of accomplishment
- Ease of formation and closure

### ❌ Limitations
- Limited resources (personal savings + borrowings only)
- Limited life of business (depends on owner's existence)
- Unlimited liability
- Limited managerial ability (one person handles all functions)

> 💡 Best suited for: **small-scale businesses & personalised services** (beauty parlours, retail shops)

## 2. 🏠 Joint Hindu Family Business

> Business owned and carried on by members of a **Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)**.  
> Governed by **Hindu Law** | Eldest member = **Karta** | Members = **Co-parceners**

### Features
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Formation | Min. 2 members + ancestral property; NO agreement needed; membership by **birth** |
| Governed by | Hindu Succession Act, 1956 |
| Liability | Karta = **Unlimited** | Other members = **Limited** (to their share) |
| Control | Karta takes all decisions; binding on others |
| Continuity | Continues after Karta's death (next eldest becomes Karta) |
| Minor Members | Minors CAN be members (membership by birth) |

### ✅ Merits
- Effective control (no conflict; Karta decides)
- Continued business existence
- Limited liability of members (except Karta)
- Increased loyalty and cooperation

### ❌ Limitations
- Limited resources (depends on ancestral property)
- Unlimited liability of Karta
- Dominance of Karta (may cause conflict)
- Limited managerial skills of Karta

> 📉 On the decline due to decreasing number of joint Hindu families in India.

## 3. 🤝 Partnership

> Defined by **Indian Partnership Act, 1932** as:  
> *"The relation between persons who have agreed to share the profit of the business carried on by all or any one of them acting for all."*

### Features
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Formation | Governed by Indian Partnership Act, 1932; formed via **legal agreement** |
| Liability | **Unlimited** – jointly AND individually liable |
| Risk Bearing | Shared among partners in agreed ratio |
| Decision Making | Joint; mutual consent |
| Continuity | **Lacks continuity** – ends on death/retirement/insolvency/insanity of a partner |
| No. of Partners | Min: **2** | Max: **50** (as per Companies Misc. Rules 2014) |
| Mutual Agency | Every partner is both **agent** and **principal** |

### ✅ Merits
- Ease of formation and closure
- Balanced decision making (partners have different expertise)
- More funds than sole proprietorship
- Sharing of risks
- Secrecy (not required to publish accounts)

### ❌ Limitations
- Unlimited liability (joint and several)
- Limited resources (max 50 partners)
- Possibility of conflicts
- Lack of continuity
- Lack of public confidence

---

### 3.1 Types of Partners

| Type | Capital | Management | Profit/Loss Share | Liability |
|------|---------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Active** | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | Unlimited |
| **Sleeping/Dormant** | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ | Unlimited |
| **Secret** | ✅ | ✅ (secretly) | ✅ | Unlimited |
| **Nominal** | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ (generally) | Unlimited |
| **Partner by Estoppel** | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | Unlimited |
| **Partner by Holding Out** | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | Unlimited |

> **Partner by Estoppel** – Person who gives impression of being a partner through own conduct/behaviour.  
> **Partner by Holding Out** – Person who knowingly allows himself to be represented as a partner.

### Minor as Partner
- Cannot become a full partner (incompetent to contract)
- Can be **admitted to benefits** with consent of all partners
- Liability limited to capital contributed
- Cannot participate in management
- On attaining majority → must decide within **6 months**; if no notice given → becomes full partner with unlimited liability

---

### 3.2 Types of Partnerships

**On basis of Duration:**
- **Partnership at Will** – Exists as long as partners want; ends when any partner gives withdrawal notice
- **Particular Partnership** – Formed for a specific project/time period; dissolves on completion

**On basis of Liability:**
- **General Partnership** – All partners have unlimited and joint liability; registration optional
- **Limited Partnership** – At least one partner has unlimited liability; rest may have limited liability; limited partners cannot manage; registration **compulsory**

---

### 3.3 Partnership Deed
Written agreement containing: name of firm, nature & location of business, duration, capital contribution, profit/loss ratio, duties, salaries, admission/retirement/expulsion terms, dissolution procedure, dispute resolution method.

### 3.4 Registration
- **Optional** but highly advisable
- Unregistered firm **cannot**:
  - File suit against the firm or other partners
  - File suit against third parties
  - File case against partners

## 4. 🤲 Cooperative Society

> Voluntary association of persons for mutual welfare; driven by **service motive**.  
> Registration **compulsory**.

### Features
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Membership | Voluntary; open to all (any religion, caste, gender) |
| Legal Status | Compulsory registration; separate legal entity |
| Liability | **Limited** (to capital contributed) |
| Control | Elected **managing committee**; principle of **"one man one vote"** |
| Service Motive | Welfare of members; surplus distributed as dividend |

### ✅ Merits
- Equality in voting (one man, one vote)
- Limited liability
- Stable existence (not affected by death/insanity of members)
- Economy in operations (eliminates middlemen)
- Government support (low taxes, subsidies, low interest loans)
- Ease of formation

### ❌ Limitations
- Limited resources (low dividend deters investment)
- Inefficiency in management (honorary/unpaid volunteers manage)
- Lack of secrecy (open discussions + disclosure obligations)
- Government control (heavy compliance requirements)
- Differences of opinion among members

---

### 4.1 Types of Cooperative Societies

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Consumer's** | Protect consumers; buy in bulk from wholesalers; eliminate middlemen |
| **Producer's** | Protect small producers; supply raw materials; buy members' output |
| **Marketing** | Help small producers sell products; pools output; handles transport/warehousing |
| **Farmer's** | Protect farmers; provide seeds/fertilisers/machinery; large-scale farming benefits |
| **Credit** | Provide easy loans at low interest; protect from exploitative lenders |
| **Housing** | Help low-income people construct houses at reasonable cost; instalment option |

## 5. 🏢 Joint Stock Company

> An **artificial person** with separate legal entity, perpetual succession and a common seal.  
> Governed by **Companies Act, 2013**.

### Features
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Artificial Person | Created by law; can own property, sue/be sued; cannot breathe/eat/run |
| Separate Legal Entity | Distinct from its members from day of incorporation |
| Formation | Complex, time-consuming, expensive; compulsory incorporation |
| Perpetual Succession | Continues regardless of members' death/exit; ends only by **winding up** |
| Control | **Board of Directors** manages; shareholders elect the board |
| Liability | **Limited** – only unpaid share amount |
| Risk Bearing | Spread over large number of shareholders |

### ✅ Merits
- Limited liability
- Easy transfer of interest (shares freely transferable in public company)
- Perpetual existence
- Scope for expansion (large capital from public)
- Professional management

### ❌ Limitations
- Complexity in formation
- Lack of secrecy (public disclosure required)
- Impersonal work environment
- Numerous regulations (SEBI, Registrar, audits, reports)
- Delay in decision making
- Oligarchic management (directors may act against shareholders' interests)
- Conflict of interests (employees vs shareholders vs consumers)

---

### 5.1 Private vs Public Company

| Basis | Public Company | Private Company |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| Min. Members | 7 | 2 |
| Max. Members | Unlimited | 200 (excl. past/present employees) |
| Min. Directors | 3 | 2 |
| Max. Directors | 15 | 15 |
| Index of Members | Compulsory | Not compulsory |
| Transfer of Shares | No restriction | Restricted |
| Public Invitation | Can invite public | Cannot invite public |
| Name suffix | "Limited" | "Private Limited" |

> A **private company subsidiary of a public company** is also treated as a **public company**.

## 6. ⚖️ Choice of Form of Organisation

| Factor | Most Advantageous | Least Advantageous |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| Availability of Capital | Company | Sole Proprietorship |
| Cost of Formation | Sole Proprietorship | Company |
| Ease of Formation | Sole Proprietorship | Company |
| Transfer of Ownership | Company (except private) | Partnership |
| Managerial Skills | Company | Sole Proprietorship |
| Regulations | Sole Proprietorship | Company |
| Flexibility | Sole Proprietorship | Company |
| Continuity | Company | Sole Proprietorship |
| Liability | Company | Sole Proprietorship |

### Key Factors to Consider:
1. **Cost & ease of setup** – Sole proprietorship cheapest; Company most expensive
2. **Liability** – Company & cooperative = limited; Sole prop & partnership = unlimited
3. **Continuity** – Company & cooperative most stable; Sole prop most unstable
4. **Management ability** – Company (professionals); Sole prop (one person does all)
5. **Capital** – Company can raise most; Sole prop most limited
6. **Degree of control** – Want full control → sole prop; Okay sharing → partnership/company
7. **Nature of business** – Personal contact needed → sole prop; Large manufacturing → company; Professional services → partnership

## 📝 Key Terms to Remember

- **Karta** – Eldest member (male or female after 2005 Amendment) who controls JHF business
- **Co-parceners** – Members of JHF with equal ownership rights over ancestral property
- **Mutual Agency** – Every partner is both agent and principal
- **Perpetual Succession** – Company continues despite changes in membership
- **Artificial Person** – Company exists in law; has rights but not physical existence
- **Partnership Deed** – Written document specifying all terms of partnership
- **Winding Up** – Legal procedure to close/dissolve a company
