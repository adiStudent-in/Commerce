# Chapter 3 — Private, Public and Global Enterprises
**Class 11 Business Studies (NCERT)**

---

## 1. Private Sector Enterprises

**Definition:** A business unit owned, controlled, and managed by private individuals or a group of individuals.

**Key Features**
- Ownership and capital come from private individuals / groups
- Managed by owners or professional managers hired by them
- Primary motive = **profit maximisation** (along with growth & goodwill)
- Decisions taken independently by the owners / Board
- Subject only to general laws of the country (e.g., Companies Act, taxes)
- No direct government interference in day-to-day working

**Examples:** Tata Sons, Reliance Industries, Aditya Birla Group, ITC, Hindustan Unilever, Mahindra & Mahindra, Infosys.

---

## 2. Public Sector Enterprises

**Definition:** A business unit owned, controlled, and managed by the government — Central, State or Local body.

**Background Factors for Growth**
- Need for rapid industrialisation
- Balanced regional development
- Control of commanding heights of the economy
- Public welfare & equitable distribution of wealth
- Self-reliance in key sectors
- Industrial Policy Resolutions of **1948** and **1956** gave major thrust

**Key Features**
- Government is the major stake-holder
- Aims at **public welfare / service motive**, not just profit
- Often pioneer sectors — heavy, basic & defence industries
- Provides goods & services at reasonable prices
- Source of revenue to government
- Supports weaker sections (e.g., reservations in employment)

**Examples:** Indian Railways, LIC, ONGC, NTPC, BHEL, SAIL, Coal India.

---

## 3. Forms of Public Sector Enterprises

There are **three** major forms:

### A. Departmental Undertakings

**Definition:** Public enterprises set up as part of a Government Department and run by government officials (civil servants).

**Key Features**
1. Established as a wing of a Government department / ministry
2. Financed from the **annual government budget**
3. Employees are **government servants** (paid as per government rules)
4. **No separate legal entity** — the department itself is the enterprise
5. Directly accountable to the concerned Minister, who is responsible to Parliament
6. Subject to **budgetary, audit & parliamentary control**
7. Annual statement of accounts is laid before Parliament

**Examples:** Indian Railways, Postal Department, Defence, Currency & Coinage, All India Radio, Doordarshan, Government Printing Press.

**Advantages**
- Direct control ensures accountability
- Suitable for **strategic / essential / defence-related** services
- Secure employment for staff
- Income & expenditure already reflected in government budget

**Disadvantages**
- Slow decisions — red-tapism & bureaucratic delays
- Lack of flexibility & autonomy
- Inefficient and time-consuming procedures
- No professional or business-like approach
- Limited scope for innovation

---

### B. Statutory Corporations

**Definition:** A public enterprise created by a **Special Act of Parliament** (or State Legislature), having a separate legal entity.

**Key Features**
1. Created by a **Special Act** of Parliament / State Legislature
2. **Separate legal entity** — can sue and be sued in its own name
3. Government provides initial capital; can also raise its own funds
4. **Autonomous in day-to-day operations**
5. Managed by a Board of Directors / Governing Body nominated by the Government
6. Employees are **not** government servants
7. Subject to **legislative & judicial control**, not direct executive control
8. Power, duties & scope clearly defined in the parent Act

**Examples:** LIC (Life Insurance Corporation), ONGC, SBI (State Bank of India), FCI (Food Corporation of India), IDBI, UTI, Indian Airlines (older).

**Advantages**
- Operates with flexibility & autonomy
- Better quality of management & professionalism
- Faster decisions than departmental undertakings
- Can raise its own funds through public deposits / debentures
- Marketing & pricing flexibility

**Disadvantages**
- Power may be misused by the Board of Directors
- Scope for favouritism, nepotism & corruption
- Sometimes does not take timely decisions on critical matters
- Persistent interference from the concerned Ministry

---

### C. Government Companies

**Definition:** A company registered under the Companies Act, 2013 in which **at least 51 % of the paid-up share capital** is held by:
- Central Government, or
- State Government, or
- Any combination of the two, or
- One or more Government companies.

**Key Features**
1. Registered under the **Companies Act**
2. **≥ 51 % shareholding** by government (Central / State / quasi-government bodies)
3. Can be a Public Limited or Private Limited Company
4. Has a **separate legal entity**
5. Operates like any other joint-stock company (subject to Companies Act)
6. Managed by a Board of Directors appointed by shareholders (mostly government)
7. Funded by share capital, government grants, borrowings & retained earnings

**Examples:** NTPC, BHEL, SAIL, BEL (Bharat Electronics), GAIL, Coal India, Indian Oil Corporation, HAL, Air India (after takeover).

**Advantages**
- Fast, professional decisions
- High operational & financial flexibility
- Easy to privatise (just transfer shares)
- Scope for better utilisation of resources
- Accountability through audit & annual reports

**Disadvantages**
- Often used for political rather than business objectives
- Inefficiency due to bureaucratic habits
- No competitive pressure → may perform poorly
- Survival becomes difficult if government withdraws support
- Difficult to wind up once set up

---

### Comparison — Three Forms of Public Sector Enterprises

| Basis | Departmental Undertaking | Statutory Corporation | Government Company |
|---|---|---|---|
| Establishing Instrument | Government order / resolution | Special Act of Parliament | Registered under Companies Act |
| Legal Status | No separate entity | Separate legal body | Separate legal body |
| Source of Capital | Government budget | Government + own funds | Share capital (≥ 51 % govt) |
| Managerial Control | Civil servants (full govt control) | Board of Directors (autonomy) | Board of Directors (autonomy) |
| Mode of Operation | Government rules & procedures | As defined in parent Act | As per Companies Act |
| Audit By | CAG | CAG | CAG + Private Auditors |
| Example | Indian Railways, Posts | LIC, SBI, ONGC | NTPC, BHEL, SAIL |

## 4. Global Enterprises (Multinational Corporations)

**Definition:** An enterprise that **operates in more than one country** with its home base in one country and branches / subsidiaries / affiliates in others. Also called *Multinational Company (MNC)* or *Transnational Company (TNC)*.

**Key Features**
1. Operates in many countries through branches / subsidiaries
2. Centralised ownership & control; headquarters located in the home country
3. Usually a very large industrial / business house
4. Expands through **mergers, acquisitions & takeovers**
5. Brings in **advanced technology & managerial expertise**
6. Spreads business risk across different countries
7. Uses local resources, labour & markets of host countries
8. Has significant economic & political influence
9. Promotes world-wide brand identity

**Examples:** Microsoft, Apple, Google, Coca-Cola, Nestle, Toyota, IBM, Samsung, Sony, Unilever, Procter & Gamble.

**Benefits to the Host Country**
- Brings latest technology & R&D
- Generates large-scale employment
- Raises the standard of living
- Quality goods at competitive prices
- Boosts exports & earns foreign exchange
- Provides training to local managers
- Promotes industrial growth

**Concerns / Limitations for the Host Country**
- May drain resources & repatriate profits
- Local competition may be crushed (anti-competitive)
- Threat to domestic industries / small firms
- Cultural & political interference
- May ignore environmental / labour norms
- Limited transfer of core / latest technology

---

## 5. Forms in Which Global Enterprises Operate

| Form | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Branch / Subsidiary** | MNC opens a branch or makes a separate subsidiary in the host country for manufacturing / selling. |
| **Joint Venture** | Two or more parties (often a domestic & foreign firm) pool resources & share ownership for a common goal. |
| **Licensing** | MNC allows a host-country firm to use its patent, brand or technology for a fee / royalty. |
| **Franchising** | MNC gives another firm the right to use its brand & business model in return for fees. |
| **Strategic Alliance** | Two or more firms cooperate on a specific project while remaining independent. |
| **Public–Private Partnership (PPP)** | Government and private firm jointly develop / operate public infrastructure. |

**Examples of Joint Ventures in India**
- Maruti Suzuki (Suzuki + GOI)
- Hero Honda
- Tata Starbucks
- Tata Volkswagen
- IBM India
- Mahindra Renault
- Air India + Singapore Airlines (Tata-SIA)

**Examples of Franchise models in India**
- McDonald's, Subway, Pizza Hut, KFC, Dominos, Baskin-Robbins.

---

## 6. Public–Private Partnership (PPP)

**Definition:** A long-term contractual arrangement in which a **government agency** and a **private company** collaborate to design, build, finance, operate and/or maintain infrastructure or public services.

**Common PPP Models**
- **BOT** — Build, Operate, Transfer
- **BOOT** — Build, Own, Operate, Transfer
- **BOO** — Build, Own, Operate
- **LOMT** — Lease, Operate, Maintain, Transfer
- **OMT** — Operate, Maintain, Transfer

**Examples:** Delhi Metro (partial PPP), Mumbai International Airport, National Highways (NHAI), Bangalore Metro, Hyderabad Metro.

## 7. Comparison — Private Sector vs Public Sector

| Basis | Private Sector | Public Sector |
|---|---|---|
| Ownership | Private individuals / groups | Central / State / Local Govt |
| Motive | Profit | Public welfare + Profit |
| Capital | Private savings, capital market | Govt treasury & capital market |
| Management | Owners / professionals | Govt officials / Board of Directors |
| Areas | Consumer & light goods | Heavy, basic & strategic sectors |
| Example | Tata, Reliance, Birla | Railways, LIC, NTPC |

---

## 8. Comparison — Public Sector Enterprise vs Global Enterprise

| Basis | Public Sector Enterprise | Global Enterprise (MNC) |
|---|---|---|
| Ownership | Government | Private (usually foreign) |
| Objective | Public welfare + profit | Profit maximisation |
| Area of Operation | Within own country | Across multiple countries |
| Control | Centralised (Government) | Headquarters in home country |
| Audit / Accountability | Govt + CAG | Mostly private audit (less govt influence) |
| Examples | NTPC, SAIL, BHEL | Microsoft, Nestle, Coca-Cola |

## 9. Key Terms — Quick Reference

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Privatisation** | Transfer of ownership from public sector to private sector |
| **Disinvestment** | Government selling its stake in a public enterprise |
| **Liberalisation** | Removal of unnecessary controls / restrictions on business |
| **Globalisation** | Integration of the domestic economy with the world economy |
| **MNC / TNC** | Multinational / Transnational Company |
| **Joint Venture** | Two or more parties pooling resources for mutual benefit |
| **PPP** | Public-Private Partnership |
| **CAG** | Comptroller and Auditor General of India |

---

## 10. Chapter at a Glance

- **Private sector** — privately owned, profit-driven, minimum govt interference.
- **Public sector** — government owned, service + welfare motive.
- **Three forms of Public sector** — Departmental Undertakings (no separate entity), Statutory Corporations (created by Special Act, separate body), Government Companies (registered under Companies Act, ≥ 51 % govt holding).
- **Global Enterprises / MNCs** — operate in multiple countries; bring technology, jobs, capital, but also raise concerns about competition, profit repatriation and political influence.
- **PPPs** — joint arrangements of government + private firms for infrastructure and public services.

## 11. Exam-Ready Answer Pattern

When asked to "Distinguish between…", structure the answer as:

> **Basis | Private Sector | Public Sector** — give 4-5 points with headings.

When asked to "Discuss advantages / disadvantages of any form of public enterprise", structure as:

> One short intro line → list of points under clear headings → two real-life examples.

When asked about **Global Enterprises / MNCs**, mention features → benefits to host country → at least 3 concerns → example.
